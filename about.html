About

Caitlin Wingerd is a Ph.D. candidate in Microbiology at The Ohio State University, where she conducts research under the mentorship of Justin North. She earned her M.S. in Microbiology from Ohio State in 2024 and her B.S. in Biology from Linfield University in 2020. Her research focuses on microbial metabolism, with particular interest in how bacteria adapt their metabolic pathways across diverse environments.

Following her undergraduate studies, Caitlin began her career in industry at Element Materials Technology (VR Analytical), where she evaluated the safety of medical plastics in drug product applications. In this role, she developed hands-on experience with analytical instrumentation, Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) standards, and client-facing data reporting.

In 2022, Caitlin joined the Microbiology Ph.D. program at Ohio State to further develop her scientific and leadership skills. She is particularly motivated to apply her combined industry and academic training to lead research initiatives, solve complex problems, and contribute to data-driven decision-making. Her experience has reinforced the importance of approaching scientific challenges with creativity, technical rigor, and a strong foundation in critical thinking.
